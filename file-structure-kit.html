Why 7 folders? The human brain processes 5-9 categories optimally. These are function-based (what you DO with files) not topic-based (what files are ABOUT). This eliminates "where does this go?" decisions.

What Goes Where

00_Active_Projects

Current initiatives with deadlines. Time-bound work. Moved to archive when complete.

01_Clients

One subfolder per client. All client materials in one place. Contracts, deliverables, communications, invoices.

02_Operations

SOPs, templates, processes. Internal systems documentation. Recurring workflows.

03_Team

HR documents, meeting notes, internal communications. Team policies and procedures.

04_Finance

Invoices, receipts, statements, tax documents, budgets. Organized by year and quarter.

05_Marketing

Assets, campaigns, analytics, brand materials. Everything external-facing.

ARCHIVE

Completed projects, inactive clients, old versions. Anything over 1 year old moves here.

The Naming Convention

The Formula:

YYYY-MM-DD_Category_Description_Version

2025-01-15_Contract_Acme_Corp_v2
2025-01_Invoice_Client_ABC_final
2025-Q1_Report_Sales_Performance

‚Ä¢Dates first (always): Files sort chronologically automatically

‚Ä¢No spaces (use underscores): Prevents issues across different systems

‚Ä¢Descriptive but concise: Should know what it is without opening it

‚Ä¢Version at end: v1, v2, v3 or draft, final, approved
