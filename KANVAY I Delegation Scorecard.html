Delegation Readiness Scorecard

Most delegation fails not because of the person, but because the system wasn't ready. This shows you where.

How It Works

Answer 15 questions across three categories: Information Architecture, Communication Systems, and Delegation Infrastructure.

Score each question:

0 = No/Never | 1 = Sometimes/Partially | 2 = Yes/Always

Section 01

Information Architecture

1. Can someone find any file in your system in under 30 seconds without asking you?

2. Is there a clear naming convention that anyone can follow?

3. Do you have documented folder logic (what goes where and why)?

4. Can you grant access to your systems without needing to explain how they're organized?

5. Could you go on vacation for 2 weeks and someone could still find everything?

Section 02

Communication Systems

6. Does your inbox have clear categories/labels that route emails automatically?

7. Can someone else tell what's urgent vs what can wait just by looking at your inbox?

8. Do you have templates for common responses?

9. Is there a clear system for tracking "waiting on someone else" items?

10. Could an assistant process 80% of your inbox without asking you questions?

Section 03

Delegation Infrastructure

11. Do you have written SOPs for recurring tasks?

12. Is there a clear handoff process when you delegate something?

13. Do you have a system that shows what's in progress vs what's complete?

14. Can you track delegated items without relying on memory?

15. Could a new assistant understand your systems in under 2 hours?
