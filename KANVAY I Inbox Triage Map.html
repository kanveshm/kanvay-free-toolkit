The Inbox Triage Map

Most inbox chaos isn't about volume. It's about decision fatigue. This system eliminates that.

The Problem

Every email forces a micro-decision: Is this urgent? Should I reply now? Where does this go? When your system can't answer those questions automatically, your inbox becomes a full-time job.

The solution isn't discipline or willpower. It's structure.

The 4-Box System

ACTION NOW

  • Requires reply within 24 hours
  • Blocks someone else's work
  • Time-sensitive deadline
  • Client-facing urgency
Reply & archive

ACTION LATER

  • Needs response but not urgent
  • Can be batched with similar tasks
  • Requires thoughtful reply
  • No immediate deadline
Move to folder

WAITING

  • You delegated this task
  • Ball is in their court
  • Needs tracking for follow-up
  • Awaiting someone's response
Track it

ARCHIVE

  • FYI only, no action needed
  • Receipt or record keeping
  • Newsletter or digest
  • Already handled elsewhere
Archive immediately

The Decision Gates

Follow this logic for every new email:

1

Does this require MY action?

NO → Archive immediately

YES → Continue to step 2

2

Is someone blocked or waiting on me?

YES → Action Now

NO → Continue to step 3

3

Did I delegate this or am I waiting on someone?

YES → Waiting

NO → Continue to step 4

4

Response needed within 24 hours?

YES → Action Now

NO → Action Later

Maintenance Rules

•Action Now: Process twice daily (10am, 4pm)

•Action Later: Batch once daily (end of day)

•Waiting: Review every Monday morning

•Archive: Do it aggressively and immediately
